Case study: Commonwealthfelt.com

Overview

Commonwealthfelt.com is the retail site for the National Nonwovens textile manufacturing corporation. Founded in 1905 as the National Felt Company, they have a long history of high quality felts and have been supplying material to the U.S. government since World War I. Being a manufacturer, National Nonwovens typically goes to market via sales representatives and wholesale companies. Today, like most American manufacturers, they are starting to target small volume consumers such as crafters and boutique businesses who specialize in handmade goods. It is this desire to sell small volumes of product to a large number of consumers that created the Commonwealthfelt.com website

The user personas reflect two types of customers most intereseted in buying felt online. An older more experienced crafter who doesn't have access to quality material and a younger, more entrepreneurial user who is comfortable with tech.

Testing

The test script was designed to simulate the tasks required in order to buy the products needed for an actual felt pattern. Test subjects were chosen based on thier simularities to the personas. I recorded the tests so I could concentrate on answering questions and maintaining the comfort level of the participants. This also allowed me to fully concentrate on observing the actions and verbal cues at a later time without distraction, as well as being a reusable resource.
